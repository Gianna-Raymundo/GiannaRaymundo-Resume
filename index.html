Hello! I'm Gianna Raymundo, a fourth year undergraduate student pursuing a Bachelor's of Science in Pure Chemistry at the University of California Riverside.

I became interested in chemistry because I admire how investigative the field is; how chemists look for answers to explain the mechanisms of everyday reactions. I enjoy studying chemistry because I love learning about different interactions between molecules and identifying the composition of various compounds. I work in Dr. Ryan Julian's lab that explores how the spontaneous isomerization of long-lived proteins in our bodies can lead to lysosmal dysfunction and presumably diseases like Alzheimer's Dementia. I synthesize peptide sequences that are found in the genome of differents animals, identify the desired peptide using mass spectrometry, purify the analyte using high performance liquid chromatography, and analyze the peptide fragmentations using methods such as: CID and RDD. My love of chemistry and teaching background in mathematics drew me to volunteer in an analytical chemistry lab.

Outside of school, I work as a Certified Pharmacy Technician at Walgreens where I serve my local community through filling prescriptions, sorting out insurance issues, dispensing medications, and administrating Flu and COVID-19 vaccines. I became a licensed and certified pharmacy technician during my third year of undergraduate study to explore my interest in the pharmaceutical industry and build off my knowledge of chemistry from academics. After working at my pharmacy for a few months, I wanted to become more involved in improving the health of my community so I took courses to become a certified immunizer. With my background in chemistry from academics and my pharmacy work experience, I hope to pursue further education in chemistry in order to work in the pharmaceutical industry.
